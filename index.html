Discover Northern Spain

Explore the green landscapes, historic cities, and rugged coastlines north of Madrid

Why Visit Northern Spain?

Northern Spain offers a very different experience from the sunny Mediterranean south. Expect cooler, lush, and often misty landscapes; charming historic cities; rugged coastlines; and a rich culinary heritage—from pintxos in the Basque Country to cider and fabada in Asturias and Galicia. While the weather in April can be variable, the region bursts into vibrant life with fewer crowds and plenty of local flavor.

Weather in April

  • Variable Climate: Expect mild days (around 15–20°C/59–68°F) and cool nights with occasional rain showers.
  • Lush & Green: Spring brings blossoming meadows and a vibrant green countryside.
  • Layer Up: Pack layers and a light raincoat to stay comfortable while you explore.

Top Regions & Attractions North of Madrid

Castile & León

Home to historic cities such as Burgos and León, this region offers Gothic cathedrals, medieval streets, and a rich cultural heritage. Wander through the ancient plazas and sample hearty regional cuisine.

The Basque Country

With renowned cities like San Sebastián, Bilbao, and charming coastal villages (think San Juan de Gaztelugatxe), the Basque Country is famous for its culinary delights, modern art (the Guggenheim in Bilbao), and rugged coastline.

Cantabria

Experience Santander’s Belle Époque charm, visit the well-preserved medieval town of Santillana del Mar, and marvel at prehistoric cave art at Altamira.

Asturias

Discover the natural beauty of Asturias in cities like Oviedo and Gijón, and explore the spectacular Picos de Europa—with highlights such as the Ruta del Cares and Lagos de Covadonga.

Galicia

From the pilgrimage city of Santiago de Compostela to the vibrant port city of A Coruña and the relaxing Rías Baixas coastal inlets, Galicia offers a mix of history, seafood feasts, and Celtic charm.

What to Do in Northern Spain

  • Cultural Tours: Wander through medieval streets in Burgos, León, or Santiago de Compostela.
  • Gastronomy: Enjoy pintxos in San Sebastián, hearty stews in Castile & León, and fresh seafood in Galicia.
  • Outdoor Adventures: Hike the Ruta del Cares in the Picos de Europa, cycle along scenic coastal roads, or kayak along rugged estuaries.
  • City Exploration: Immerse yourself in the modern art and vibrant nightlife of Bilbao, or stroll the charming promenades of A Coruña.

Planning Your Trip

  • Mix Your Itinerary: Combine cultural city breaks with days exploring nature in remote villages and mountain trails.
  • Transportation: Renting a car is ideal for reaching off-the-beaten-path destinations, while intercity buses and trains connect major urban areas.
  • Pack Wisely: Bring layers and rain gear to be ready for variable April weather.
